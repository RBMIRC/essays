The Aristotelian conception of making where form preexists in the creator's mind and is then imposed on passive matter. The model of the engineer, the industrial designerâ€”and of generative AI, which "applies" a prompt to statistical matter. Tim Ingold opposes this to *correspondence*: making as dialogue with materials rather than imposition upon them. The hylomorphic model assumes the maker knows what they want before they begin; correspondence discovers what is possible through the encounter. (*The Workshop as Form*)
