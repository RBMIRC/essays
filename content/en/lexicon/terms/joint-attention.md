Matthew Crawford's term: two people oriented together toward the same object, each perceiving the other's attention to it. Pedagogically powerful because it shows not only *what* to look at but *how* to look. The student learns to see what counts by seeing what the teacher looks atâ€”attending not only to the object but to the quality and rhythm of the teacher's attention to the object. Joint attention cannot be digitally mediated in the same way; it requires shared perceptual space. (*The Workshop as Form*)
