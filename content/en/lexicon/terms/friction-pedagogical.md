Author's term: resistance that forces thought. Not necessarily physicalâ€”the bug one does not understand, the archive that does not answer the question, the algorithm producing aberrant results are all frictions. What distinguishes frictions pedagogically is their legibility: the friction of wood is immediately perceptible (one sees the resistance, feels the necessary force); the friction of the algorithm is often opaque (the result is there but the reasons are hidden). The teacher's task includes making invisible frictions visible. (*The Workshop as Form*)
