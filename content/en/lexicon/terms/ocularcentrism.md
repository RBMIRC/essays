Martin Jay's term for the privileging of vision in Western thought. To understand is to see; to explain is to show; to think is to render visible. The diagram inscribes itself in this economy of visibility, promising to make visible what was obscureâ€”but some things resist being mapped. The well cannot be diagrammed without becoming a line; the vertical cannot be represented without being flattened. Ocularcentrism forecloses what cannot be seen. (*Nodal Thinking*)
