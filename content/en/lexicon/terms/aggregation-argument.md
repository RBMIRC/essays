Classical critique holding that computation accomplishes only aggregation of preexisting elements, not the emergent synthesis characterizing authentic collective creation. Theodor Adorno and Max Horkheimer saw the culture industry as a machine for combining standardized elements—producing pseudo-individuality through mechanical recombination of interchangeable parts. Contemporary theorists contest this view, arguing computation and collective creativity are potentially complementary rather than opposed. (*The Recursive Archive*)
