Discourses on the digital often invoke sovereignty—temporal sovereignty (control over one's time), creative sovereignty (control over one's production), subjective sovereignty (control over one's attention). But sovereignty presupposes a unified subject, master of themselves. Everything we know about technics shows the subject is always-already constituted by the dispositifs that traverse it—we become who we are through tools, languages, institutions we did not create. Better to speak of *capabilities*: not mastery but capacity for action, not sovereignty but agency within constraints. (*The Workshop as Form*)
