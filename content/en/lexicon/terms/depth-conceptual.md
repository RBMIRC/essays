Author's term: a quality of cognitive experience where a concept resists immediate grasping, forcing return and reformulation. A concept has depth when it does not let itself be captured in a formula, when each encounter reveals new aspects, when understanding deepens rather than completing. The diagram, by its nature, tends to evacuate depthâ€”it produces fluid connections, frictionless passages between nodes. What gets lost is the vertical dimension: the labor of dwelling with difficulty rather than moving past it. (*Nodal Thinking*)
