Bruno Latour and Steve Woolgar's concept from laboratory studies: the curves, tables, graphs, and diagrams that do not merely transcribe preexisting facts but participate in constituting them. The instrument produces an inscription; the inscription becomes evidence; accumulated evidence becomes fact. It is the inscriptions themselves that, through stabilization and serialization, eventually acquire the status of facts. The diagram is such an inscriptionâ€”not recording thought but producing it. (*Nodal Thinking*)
