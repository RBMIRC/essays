Tim Ingold's term: learning to perceive what the material tells us, to adjust our gesture to its responses. Teaching cannot limit itself to transmitting ideas to be applied; it must show how the gesture responds to the material. The novice learns not by being told but by being shown, not by receiving information but by having their attention directedâ€”look here, notice this, feel how it resists. Attention is not a faculty we possess but a skill we cultivate. (*The Workshop as Form*)
